Talking about Moon Race, who do you think is having an upper edge, USA
or the Soviet Union. Now let me take you through some fascinating facts which
pioneered the impossible to possible. Let me introduce ‘SPUTNIK’ the world’s
first artificial satellite which was launched by the Soviet Union in 1957,
making them a space research giants.

 

The ‘LUNA 2’ was the first spacecraft to reach the Lunar orbit launched
again by the Soviet Union in 1959. But ‘LUNA 9’ of the ‘LAVOCHKIN’ which is the
space agency of the Soviet Union was the first spacecraft to land on the moon
surface. The US, though had lots of unsuccessful attempts, Apollo 8 which was
the first crewed mission which was successful in reaching the moon’s orbit but
failed to bring the crew members back as engine failed while returning to earth.

Obviously USSR and US were in a cold-war in terms of the space research,
here the Russians even though had many unsuccessful attempts initially but
managed to bite the dust and proved that they were more advanced and developed
than the United States in the case of space research.

In 1969, history was made by NASA, Neil Aiden
Armstrong of Apollo 11 was the first person to land on the moon. This
successful mission was followed by Apollo 12,13,14 etc.. all turned up to be
successful. During 1970-75 period, NASA and USSR joined forces together and
guess what…!!! they created an ‘INTERNATIONAL SPACE STATION’ which was used to
dock the carriers.

This great achievements by the two nations had set up a bar for other countries
to take space research more seriously. Japan, after unsuccessful attempts had
joined forces with the NASA and successfully launched ‘CLEMENTINE’. This paved
way for Japan to start their own research organization called ‘ISAS’, later
renamed as Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency(JAXA).

 

Soon, the European Union decided to build their own space research
organization and there it is.. ‘European Space Agency’ was created comprising
of more than 20 European countries. Their First mission being ‘SMART-1’ was
successful.

 

The Chinese who always wanted to become the superpower created their own
space research agency named ‘CHINA NATIONAL SPACE ADMINISTRATION’. They
launched their lunar orbiter ‘CHANG’LE-1’ in 2007 which was successful. They
had claimed that their space carrier was cheapest of all the launched space
vehicles.

Out of all odds, here comes India, a developing country which has a
poverty of 28% into the battle between the developed nations. Guess what, we
build the cheapest lunar orbiter ever made, even giants like NASA, ESA,
LAVOCHKIN couldn’t design a cheap launch vehicle. The ‘INDIAN SPACE RESEARCH
ORGANIZATION(ISRO)’ is India’s space agency and history was made. The
‘CHANDRAYAAN-1’ was the first lunar mission of ISRO and it was successful.
Using Chandrayaan, we discovered ‘water ice’ on the surface of the moon.

 

A small private agency called LuxSpace in Luxembourg, had joined the
party with a successful misson ‘Manfred Memorial Moon Mission’ which created a
buzz as Luxembourg is a very small country in Europe and they had scaled
something unachievable.

 

Concluding

 

As we discussed the race to the moon isn’t a matter of science but it
was a battle for prestige too. Even though USSR’s Lavochkin was the first
agency to successfully land an orbiter on the moon, the US had the upper hand
as they were the first ones to successfully land a crew orbiter and they took
the privilege of being the first agency to land a Human on

the surface of the moon. Moon project was a dream project for every
other country and soon they started their own space agencies and started to
launch their own Space vehicles. The Moon Race had set up standards for space
research and scientists are working on the settlement of humans in moons.
Whichever country discovers environment suitable for human life on the moon
will be the next super power. So the Race is on!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

Don’t worry. I am not going to make a speech from the commonreasons-

for-becoming-an-entrepreneur- list like Freedom, Selfrespect,

sense of adventure, etc.

Instead, I am going to share with you what I told my mom when

she listened to me answer briefly to the same question above for

45 minutes and said “There is only one Elon Musk who

succeeded”.

Let’s be honest. I am not a class topper or a science geek or a

Hari(the boy in my class who acts stupid but knows everything).I

am an average student…who was very scared to go out of his

comfort zone…with very supportive parents who want me to

become a normal engineer with a normal job…with huge salary.

But everything changed when one day my whole family decided

to take a vacation together at Kodaikanal.

The Kodaikanal Incident

The five-day trip which was really exciting at first suddenly crash

landed to becoming one of the worst memories I have.

That was also the first time I realized that my cousins were so

competitive that they would even calculate the fourth decimal

point of their percentage marks to determine who was

ahead….Seriously…I watched it happen.

This wasn’t the problem. The problem was when they turned their

heads towards me…I had nothing. Even the prizes that I got for

the State level arts festival didn’t cushion the blows.

Why?

Because those weren’t “achievements”.

Then they started giving me suggestions on what to become…

ranging from a bank manager to a rocket scientist.

That’s when my uncle Santosh said, “Be something that no one in

our family has ever become”.

Yeah…I am going to be someone who doesn’t care about what

their family want them to be and write my own story.

Of course I didn’t say this out loud and I think Uncle Santosh

actually meant being an astronaut or a formula one racer…but I

decided to take that suggestion into my head.

The Elon Musk factor

From dropping out of Stanford University to becoming the world’s

31st richest person and being called the “Real Life Ironman”. How

cool is that!!

Stumbling across Ashlee Vance’s biography of Elon Musk was

one of the best things that ever happened to me. Seeing how a

man with a vision, change the world with nothing but technology,

made him the John McClane to my Jake Peralta.

After reading and understanding more and more…being an

entrepreneur was like performing a magic trick to me. People first

thinking nothing was going to work and finally getting their minds

blown away…was the perfect way to show my family and the world what dreams can win.

After all…even the most selfless choices and decisions are born out of a personal experience...and success is not only born out of brilliance or determination…but out of necessity too.

Conclusion

I want to be an entrepreneur because I want to be a magician.

I want to watch people gasp as the swords go inside the box, and see them applaud as I emerge unharmed from the other end.

I want to see people laugh and feel sorry when I fall down climbing the mountain, and see them cheer for me and proudly hold my hand up when I reach the peak.

I don’t care if there’s only one Elon Musk. I chase my dreams to carve my own name on the stone…so that I can watch boys and girls like me read my story and gain strength to say, Abracadabra.
